Teenager Rose Luka Shardon Found in Critical Condition After Epping Forest Search

EPPING FOREST, ENGLAND — BY GAZETTE INVESTIGATIVE DESK

Seventeen-year-old Rose Luka Shardon was found in critical condition deep within Epping Forest on August XXth at 13:47, following a gruelling 48-hour search by local authorities. Shardon was immediately rushed to a nearby hospital where she is being treated for her injuries.

The cause of her injuries remains uncertain, and police are actively searching Epping Forest for further evidence that could lead to her assailant. Detectives involved in the police investigation have attempted to question Shardon about the incident, but she has so far been unable to provide a statement.

Medical staff at [REDACTED] Hospital have indicated that Shardon is suffering from a heavy dissociative fugue, a rare psychiatric condition often triggered by severe trauma.

"A dissociative fugue is a temporary state where a person experiences profound memory loss, not just of events, but often of their entire identity. For Rose, it's almost as if she doesn't remember who she is anymore; it's like her person has split into two."

Dr. Eleanor Vance, Head of Psychiatry at [REDACTED] Hospital, explained that during this state, individuals are typically unaware of their memory loss. "It's a profound psychological response to overwhelming stress or trauma, which in Rose's case, we believe is linked to the possible assault," Dr. Vance added.

Due to the suspected assault, Shardon is heavily traumatized and suffering significantly. Her condition is critical but stable, and she remains under constant medical and psychiatric supervision.

The family of Rose Luka Shardon has requested to remain anonymous at this time and has appealed to the media for privacy during the ongoing investigation, stating their focus is entirely on Rose's recovery.

The police have appealed to the public for any information regarding Shardon's disappearance. Anyone with relevant information is urged to contact [REDACTED]. Case Status: ONGOING
